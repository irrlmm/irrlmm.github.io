# this is personal

Not many people in the field realize this, even over the span of a career.

Around friends, I often say: _choose a product if it speaks with you_.

When I close my eyes and imagine the pantheon of products I have lived with, I see products that stay great no matter the time or place.

### \*\*\*

Welcome to the cult of everyday change.  
The age of vibe coding.

Too many things around us now are things we do not want to experience, but _have to_.  
That's what happens when anyone can pass for a developer, an engineer, or a designer.

Software is bloated with motion, while task completion gets slower. The bells and whistles get praise, then morph into complaints about friction.

Feels like a bad time to be a designer, because _too little is made to be remembered_.

### \*\*\*

In my pantheon, there's the Bic pen. The iPod Nano. The SP-404.
They are all incredibly flawed, but they got the combination right.

It starts with the idea.  
_Why should the product exist?_

_Someone_ cared enough to ask that question seriously. Understood what the thing was for, and what made it possible. Then came the look and the feel.

_Someone_ was a great product designer, no matter the formal title.

And I think: _I want to be like them_.
